Thysdrus (modern El Djem, Tunisia) was a Punic and Roman city of North Africa. It flourished in the second century AD and built its massive amphitheater in the third century. After an abortive revolt in AD 238, the city was destroyed by soldiers loyal to Maximinus Thrax.
